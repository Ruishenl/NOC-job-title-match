Develop and administer audiometric tests and examinations using specialized instruments and electronic equipment to diagnose and evaluate the degree and type of patients' hearing impairment. Plan and implement habilitation/rehabilitation programs for patients, including selection, fitting and adjustment of amplification devices, such as hearing aids, balance retraining exercises and teaching speech (lip) reading. Educate and counsel patients and families regarding the nature, extent, impact and implications of hearing loss and treatment. Establish personalized care plans working as a member of an interdisciplinary team. Conduct research related to hearing and hearing disorders. May instruct and supervise audiometric technicians, students and other health care personnel.
