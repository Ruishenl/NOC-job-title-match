Operate X-ray, radiographic and fluoroscopic equipment, computerized tomography (CT) scanners, mammography units and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scanners to produce radiographs or anatomic images of the human body for the diagnosis by radiologists of disease or injury. Record and process patient data. Perform basic verification and quality control checks on radiographic and film processing equipment. Provide appropriate care and monitoring of the patient during the radiographic examination. Explain procedures, position patient and equipment and apply radiation protection measures. May train and supervise student radiographers or supervise other radiological technologists.
